Bayern Munich extend lead

bayern - leipzig. headlights

24.04.2025

Bayerns dominance continued, and they doubled their lead just six minutes later. Konrad Laimer found himself through on goal after a brilliant pass from Serge Gnabry. The Austrian midfielder kept his composure, calmly slotting the ball past Müller to make it 2-0. Heidenheim struggled to contain Bayern's attacking prowess, with the visitors enjoying 64 per cent possession and peppering the home side's goal with shots.

The visitors wasted no time in asserting their dominance at the Voith-Arena. In the 13th minute, Kane broke the deadlock with a sublime finish. The England captain received the ball from Michael Olise on the edge of the box, turned, and rifled a shot into the net, leaving Heidenheim goalkeeper Kevin Müller with no chance. Kane's powerful strike, clocked at 118.53 km/h, marked his 24th Bundesliga goal of the season, further cementing his place at the top of the scoring charts.

The first half onslaught continued as Coman made it 3-0 in the 36th minute. A flowing move saw Kane set up Guerreiro, whose initial shot was saved by Müller. However, the rebound fell kindly to Coman, who showed great composure to find the net from a tight angle. The goal effectively ended the contest before half-time, with Heidenheim looking shell-shocked and Bayern in complete control.
